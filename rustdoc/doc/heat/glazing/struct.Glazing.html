Expand description

An abstraction of a glazing layer for optical purposes.

All properties can be Solar or Visible spectral averages, or they can be constrained to a specific wavelength. However, this library—because it is all about heat transfer—only uses them in Solar purposes

pub fn combined_tau(&self, other: &Self) -> Float

Calculates the overall transmittance of a system of two glazing layers

Source: ISO-9050/2003, Equation 2

\tau_{1-2} = \frac{\tau_1 \tau_2}{1 - \rho'_1 \rho_2}

This equation—along with the others in this module—can be used recursively, treating two layers combined as a single one and then attaching a third one. That is to say:


\tau_{1-3} = \frac{\tau_{1-2} \times \tau_3}{1 - \rho'_{1-2} \rho_3}
source

pub fn combined_rho_front(&self, other: &Self) -> Float

Calculates the overall front reflectance of a system of two glazing layers

Source: ISO-9050/2003, Equation 5

\rho_{1-2} = \rho_1 + \frac{{\tau_1}^2 \rho_2}{1 - \rho'_1 \rho_2}
source

pub fn combined_rho_back(&self, other: &Self) -> Float

Calculates the overall back reflectance of a system of two glazing layers

This equation is not explicitly written on the standard I think, but we can derive it by drawing the system and assinging the corresponding values to Equation 5 of the same standard

\rho'_{1-2} = \rho'_2 + \frac{{\tau_2}^2 \rho'_1}{1 - \rho_2 \rho'_1}

pub fn combined_alphas(&self, other: &Self) -> (Float, Float)

Calculates the front solar absorbtance of two Glazing according to Equations 17 and 18 of ISO9050/2003

The resulting absorbtances are

\alpha_{e1} = \alpha_1 + \frac{\alpha'_1 \tau_1 \rho_2 }{1 - \rho'_1 \rho_2}

and

\alpha_{e2} = \frac{\alpha_2 \tau_1}{1 - \rho'_1 \rho_2}
source

pub fn alphas(layers: &[Glazing]) -> Vec<Float>

Calculates the absorbtances of each Glazing of the system, proportional to the incident radiation (i.e., they do not add up to 1.0)

This function assumes that there is a layer of air (i.e., a cavity) between the glazing layers.
